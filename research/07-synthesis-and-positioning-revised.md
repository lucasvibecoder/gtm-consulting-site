## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The fractional GTM engineering and Clay agency market is crowded at the extremes — cheap courses at the bottom ($299-$999), $20K-$150K/month premium engagements at the top — but has a structural gap in the middle where Series A/B SaaS companies ($1M-$10M ARR) sit. Competitors either create dependency (done-for-you agencies like ColdIQ, Growth Engine X), require the client to self-educate (course-first models like Blueprint GTM), or operate at price points that exclude earlier-stage companies (Smoke Signals at $50K-$150K, Blue Modern at $30K-$150K+, The Kiln at $15K-$50K+/month). Nobody is explicitly positioning around closing the specific gap between "bought Clay" and "getting pipeline" at a price point accessible to growth-stage startups.

Lucas's combination — deep Clay expertise from working at Clay Labs + 4.3 years of SDR/AE experience carrying a quota + strong prompt engineering and signal-based workflow skills — creates a positioning advantage that's hard to replicate. The key is framing it honestly: not "I built Clay's enterprise playbooks" but "I learned this platform from the inside, I've done the sales job your reps are doing, and I build systems that actually work for the people who have to use them."

## 7. PROOF STRATEGY: Solving the Case Study Problem

You don't have production metrics from Clay Labs. That's a real constraint. Here's how to build proof without them:

### Path 1: Eat Your Own Cooking (Best Option)

**What:** Use your own Clay skills to build your own outbound pipeline. Document everything. Your first case study becomes: "How I used signal-based Clay workflows to generate my first consulting clients."

**Why this is stronger than a borrowed case study:**
- Proves the system works because you bet your livelihood on it
- Nobody can question your access to the data — it's yours
- Shows the full process, not just cherry-picked results
- Resonates with founders who respect practitioners over theorists

**How to document:**
- Screenshot your Clay tables, enrichment logic, signal detection
- Track metrics: prospects identified → outreach sent → replies → calls booked → clients signed
- Record yourself building the system (Loom or screen recording)
- Write up the case study: problem, approach, system built, results

### Path 2: First Client at Discount in Exchange for Case Study

**What:** Find one company that matches your ICP. Offer the Pipeline Sprint at $1,000-$2,000 (or free) in exchange for permission to document the engagement and results.

**How to find them:**
- Post in Clay Community, GTM Engineers Slack, or LinkedIn: "I'm offering 2 discounted Pipeline Sprints to build case studies. You get a working Clay-to-pipeline system in 2 weeks. I get permission to document the results."
- This is standard practice for new consultants — nobody will think it's weird
- Target companies where you can generate measurable results quickly (small team, clear ICP, existing Clay account)

### Path 3: Build-in-Public Demo Systems

**What:** Build a fully working Clay-to-pipeline system for a realistic scenario. Record yourself building it. Walk through every decision. Publish as content.

**Examples:**
- "How I'd build a signal-based outbound system for a Series A HR tech company" — full walkthrough
- "Enrichment pipeline architecture: from raw list to qualified, scored, sequenced prospects" — step by step
- "The prompt engineering behind AI qualification: how I'd score prospects for a fintech startup" — showing your multi-prompt approach

**Why this works:**
- Demonstrates capability without needing client permission
- Becomes LinkedIn/YouTube content that generates inbound
- Shows your process and thinking, which is what buyers actually evaluate
- Can be produced immediately — no client needed

### Recommended Approach

**Do all three in sequence:**
1. **Now:** Build your own outbound system (Path 1) — this starts generating clients AND becomes your first case study
2. **Weeks 2-3:** Create 1-2 build-in-public demo systems (Path 3) — these become your content engine
3. **When first client signs:** Offer case study exchange (Path 2) — this gives you a real client name and metrics
